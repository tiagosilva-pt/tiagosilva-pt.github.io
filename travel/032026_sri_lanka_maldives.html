Sri Lanka and Maldives - March 2026

Exploring Sri Lanka and Maldives

The trip began in Colombo, a city that immediately hits you with a sensory explosion of heat, thick aromas, and a chaotic yet strangely organized flow of life. Moving through the markets feels like diving into a sea of people and fruit, accompanied by a constant soundtrack of traffic and rhythmic honking. Our first full day was spent exploring the city’s spiritual side, visiting Buddhist sanctuaries where golden statues stand amidst delicate offerings. A stop at the National Museum provided a deeper look into the island's history, where I was struck by the numerous Portuguese, Dutch and British influences still visible today. Each day ended the same way, with a long soak in the pool to wash off the humidity of the capital.

As we moved inland with our guides, the rhythm of the trip shifted toward the lush greenery of the jungle. The road life in Sri Lanka is an adventure in itself, defined by daring overtakes and a relentless pace that eventually mellows out into the interior. We explored the Dambulla cave temples, where ancient stone Buddhas and intricate frescoes are tucked away in the rock, often under the watchful eye of local monkeys. One of the most memorable afternoons involved visiting a traditional village, where a boat ride led to a cooking lesson and a meal eaten traditionally with our hands. It was a day capped off by an incredible safari, watching elephants move through their natural habitat.

The ascent to Sigiriya felt like reaching the crown of the island, often compared to Machu Picchu for its dramatic scale and historical weight. Climbing the endless stairs in the midday heat was a physical test, but the view from the top, made every step worth it. From there, we moved to Kandy to visit the Temple of the Sacred Tooth, though the real energy was found in the frantic, winding aisles of the local market. The journey then took us even higher, reaching 2,000 meters into the tea plantations. The air turned crisp as we navigated mountain roads through Hindu and Islamic areas, touring a tea factory that felt like a relic of the British colonial era before we began our long descent toward the southern coast.

Our time in Sri Lanka wound down in the historic town of Galle, where the colonial fort meets the crashing waves of the Indian Ocean. We watched the stilt fishermen at work and took our first real dive into the sea before making the final loop back to Colombo.

The transition to the Maldives for the final leg of the trip was a total shift in frequency. The rugged exploration of the previous week was replaced by three days of pure serenity. The routine became beautifully simple: sun, heat, and snorkeling in water so clear it felt perfect beyond description. It was the ultimate reward after the high-energy trek through the heart of Sri Lanka.
